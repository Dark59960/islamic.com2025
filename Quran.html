Quran

Quran is the holy book of Islam. It is the word of God as revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) through the Angel Gabriel (Jibril) over a period of approximately 23 years. The Quran is written in Arabic and is considered the final revelation from God to humanity, following previous scriptures such as the Torah and the Bible. The Quran is divided into chapters called Surahs, which are then divided into verses called Ayahs. The Quran is recited and memorized by Muslims all over the world, and is considered the ultimate source of guidance for all aspects of life.

It covers various topics including theology, morality, law, and guidance for personal conduct. The Quran is also known for its literary beauty and eloquence, and is often recited in a melodious manner during prayers and other occasions. Muslims believe that the Quran is the literal word of God, and it is recited in Arabic during prayers and other religious rituals. It is also memorized by millions of Muslims around the world, and is considered a source of spiritual guidance and comfort. The Quran emphasizes the importance of faith, prayer, charity, and good deeds, and encourages believers to seek knowledge and understanding.
